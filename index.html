Essential Fives

with Transistor Studios / Häagen-Dazs

These Häagen-Dazs units were developed as part of the larger E5 campaign, encouraging users to write down their top five items in a five-year time capsule to promote the new Häagen-Dazs Five ice cream. I worked as the lead animator and developer on these ads at Transistor Studios. The animations were created using TweenLite, and the units were trafficked through PointRoll.

Quaker Experience

with Transistor Studios / Quaker Oatmeal

These Quaker Oatmeal ads were developed as part of a larger campaign encouraging users to experiment with various Oatmeal Topping Ideas. I worked as the lead Flash animator and developer on these units at Transistor Studios. I worked closely with the 3D modeling artists, compositors, and art director on the concept and development. The ads were trafficked through EyeWonder.

Castle

my Personal Project / School of Visual Arts

This interactive scene was created as a prototype for the first scene of a point-and-click fairy tale themed game. Interactions include the click and drag clouds, mouseover trees, and mouseover windmills.

Forest

my Personal Project / School of Visual Arts

This forest scene was created as a prototype for the second scene of a point-and-click fairy tale themed Flash game.

Pirates T-Shirt

my Personal Project

This shirt design was a submission for a pirates T-shirt contest. I wanted to capture the playful feeling of children playing pirates, a contrast to the other horror-themed design submissions.

Tree

my Personal Project / Cornell University

This motion graphic video was created as a Personal Project to show the effect of life to decay, using a tree as the backdrop. I created four separate drawings, and morphed them together.

Wild Swans

my MFA Thesis Project / School of Visual Arts

Wild Swans was created as my MFA thesis project in the Computer Art department at the School of Visual Arts. I illustrated and developed this augmented reality project using ActionScript 3, PaperVision3D, and FLARToolKit. In this recording, markers were printed on paper and held up to the camera, telling the fairy tale of The Wild Swans.
